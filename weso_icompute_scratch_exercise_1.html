The weather guesser

Create a Scratch program that asks the user for a temperature between 0 and 100. Based on the answer, the sprite should suggest an activity and change its costume to match the season.

  • 0–39 → say "Time to ski!" and switch to a winter backdrop
  • 40–59 → say "Great day for golf!" and switch to a spring backdrop
  • 60–79 → say "Let's play tennis!" and switch to a summer backdrop
  • 80–100 → say "Perfect for swimming!" and switch to a sunny backdrop
Note: This is very close to a real past WESO Section C question — great prep!
Standard Matches WESO competition level

The science quiz show

Create a Scratch program that runs a 3-question science quiz. Use at least two sprites — one as the "host" and one as a "contestant." The program should:

  1. Ask the player each question one at a time using ask and wait.
  2. Keep a running score — add 10 points for each correct answer.
  3. After all 3 questions, the host should announce the final score and say something different depending on how well the player did:
    • 30 points → "Perfect score! You're a science star!"
    • 10–20 points → "Nice work! Keep studying!"
    • 0 points → "Better luck next time!"
  4. Add a backdrop and background music that plays throughout.
You pick the questions! Use any three science facts you know — animals, planets, human body, etc. Keep answers simple (one word or number).
Challenge + Above & beyond — targets full creativity/efficiency points

The number guessing game

Create a Scratch program that picks a secret random number between 1 and 20, then lets the player guess until they get it right. The program should:

  1. Use pick random to choose the secret number at the start.
  2. Ask the player to guess and store the answer.
  3. Tell the player if their guess is too high, too low, or correct.
  4. Keep looping until the player guesses correctly (repeat until block).
  5. When correct, say how many guesses it took and play a victory sound.
  6. Use a variable on screen to show the number of guesses live.
Efficiency tip: Use one sprite with costumes for "happy," "confused," and "sad" to show feedback — no extra sprites needed. This is the kind of thing that earns the creativity/efficiency tiebreaker points.

This is the exact WESO 2026 Section C scoring breakdown. Use it to self-grade before competition day.

CategoryPointsWhat the judges look for
Correct algorithm 10 pts Uses the right programming constructs — if/else for decisions, loops for repetition, variables for tracking. The logic is correct even if there are small bugs.
Working program 10 pts The program actually runs and produces the correct output for the given task. No crashes.
Precision 5 pts Attention to detail — correct spelling in messages, right number of seconds, all edge cases handled (e.g. what happens at exactly 0 or exactly 100).
Creativity & efficiency 5 pts Tiebreaker. Extra sprites, costumes, sounds, clever use of loops to avoid repeated code, backdrop changes, custom blocks, etc.
Total Section C: 30 points  ·  Full test total: 50 points
Coach tip: The creativity/efficiency 5 points are the tiebreaker — they only matter when two teams are tied on everything else. Don't let students spend the bulk of their 45 minutes decorating. Algorithm + working program = 20 points. That's where to focus.
