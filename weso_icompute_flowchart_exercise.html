Quick reference — the three shapes

Oval

Only at the very beginning or end. One arrow in OR out — never both.

Rectangle

The algorithm does something. One arrow in, one arrow out.

Diamond

Asks a yes/no question. Always exactly two arrows out — labeled Yes and No.

The golden rules

Rule 1 — every arrow must lead somewhere. No arrow can point into thin air. It must connect to a shape.
Rule 2 — decisions always have exactly two exits. A diamond with one exit or three exits is wrong.
Rule 3 — start and end are ovals, not rectangles. Students commonly draw "Start" as a box — it should always be an oval.
Rule 4 — label your Yes and No arrows. Both arrows out of a decision diamond must be labeled so the reader knows which path is which.
Rule 5 — loops are fine. A "No" arrow can point back up to an earlier step. That's how you show waiting or retrying (like the toast example).
